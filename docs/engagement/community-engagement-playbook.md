## Overview

This playbook establishes repeatable patterns for authentic community participation that builds brand authority through genuine conversation, not broadcasting.

### Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Listen First** | Understand current conversations before contributing |
| **Value-Add Responses** | Share insights, not just agreement |
| **Technical Credibility** | Be precise when discussing rollup architecture, ZK proofs, or L2 design |
| **Supportive Tone** | Help newcomers, celebrate community wins, acknowledge good questions |
| **Avoid** | Shilling, repetitive content, overly promotional language |

## Discord Engagement Strategy

### Key Channels to Monitor

| Channel | Purpose | Engagement Type |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| #general | Community chat, announcements | Thoughtful replies, value-add comments |
| #developers | Technical discussions, dev support | Answer questions, share resources |
| #announcements | Official news, updates | Amplify with context |
| #memes | Community culture | Participate authentically |
| #feedback | Product feedback | Gather insights, acknowledge contributions |
| Regional channels (e.g., #中文, #한국어) | Localized discussions | Respect boundaries unless fluent |

### Participation Patterns

#### Dos ✅
- **Listen First** — Spend time understanding current conversations before contributing
- **Value-Add Responses** — Share insights, not just agreement (e.g., "This is interesting because...")
- **Technical Credibility** — When discussing rollup architecture, ZK proofs, or L2 design, be precise
- **Supportive Tone** — Help newcomers, celebrate community wins, acknowledge good questions

#### Don'ts ❌
- Shilling or overly promotional language
- Repetitive content or copy-paste responses
- Jumping into conversations without context
- Over-engaging in regional channels without fluency

### Content Angles That Resonate

1. **Technical deep-dives** — ZK-Rollup mechanics, based contests, proposer-prover games
2. **Ecosystem growth** — New dApps, partnerships, milestones
3. **Developer experience** — Tooling improvements, documentation, hackathons
4. **Community stories** — Builder spotlights, user journeys, testimonials

## Content Angles by Audience

### Developers 👨‍💻

- "How to build on Taiko" resources
- Gas optimization tips
- Smart contract deployment guides
- Hackathon announcements and recaps
- Developer tooling improvements

**Example Engagement:**
> "Great question about deployment patterns! The proposer-prover separation in Taiko means you can optimize for different gas costs compared to other L2s. Here's a quick comparison..."

### DeFi Users 💰

- Bridge tutorials and safety tips
- DApp ecosystem highlights
- Tokenomics explainers (when appropriate)
- Liquidity opportunities

**Example Engagement:**
> "For anyone bridging to Taiko — remember that based contestable rollups have different finality properties than optimistic rollups. Here's what that means for your transactions..."

### Researchers/Technical 🔬

- ZK proof system comparisons
- Based contestable rollup deep-dives
- Proposer-prover game theory
- Cryptographic innovations

**Example Engagement:**
> "This is a great thread on ZK-Rollup tradeoffs. Worth noting that Taiko's approach to based sequencing changes the MEV dynamics compared to centralized sequencers..."

### General Crypto Community 🌐

- "Why Taiko matters" narratives
- Ecosystem milestone celebrations
- Community culture and memes
- Educational content

**Example Engagement:**
> "Love seeing the community growth! The based contestable design really does change the game for decentralization. Here's a simple breakdown of why it matters..."

## Success Metrics

### Qualitative (Primary)

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Authentic engagement** | Builds trust and credibility |
| **Quality replies** | Demonstrates expertise and adds value |
| **Community trust** | Long-term relationship building |
| **Helpful contributions** | Positions Taiko as a helpful ecosystem |

### Quantitative (Secondary)

- Response time to community questions
- Engagement rate on original content
- Community sentiment indicators
- Developer onboarding completion rates

### Key Insight

> **Authentic engagement > Volume of posts**  
> **Quality replies > Quick reactions**  
> **Community trust > Follower count**  
> **Helpful contributions > Self-promotion**
